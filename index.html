Rights
Learn about Rights

Brief: Rights are fundamental to a just and equitable society for several key reasons. Firstly, they protect individual freedoms and autonomy, ensuring that people can live their lives without undue interference from others or the government. Secondly, they promote fairness and equality by guaranteeing equal treatment under the law, regardless of factors like race, gender, or religion. Thirdly, rights provide a framework for resolving disputes and conflicts peacefully, reducing the potential for violence and instability. Moreover, they empower individuals to participate in civic life, fostering democracy and civic engagement. Lastly, rights serve as a moral compass, reflecting societal values and aspirations, guiding us toward a more humane and progressive future.
